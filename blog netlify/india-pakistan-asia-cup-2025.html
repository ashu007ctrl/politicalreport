India vs Pakistan Asia Cup 2025: The Handshake Controversy

Published Date : September 17, 2025

On September 14, 2025, India delivered a masterclass performance against Pakistan in the highly anticipated Asia Cup 2025 Group A clash at Dubai International Cricket Stadium, winning by 7 wickets with 25 balls remaining.

India celebrating their comprehensive victory over Pakistan in Asia Cup 2025

Match Summary

Pakistan captain Salman Agha won the toss and elected to bat first, a decision that backfired spectacularly as India's bowling attack dismantled the Pakistani batting lineup. Pakistan could only manage 127/9 in their 20 overs, with India chasing down the target with ease, reaching 131/3 in just 15.5 overs.

Pakistan's Batting Collapse

India struck early and devastatingly through their pace attack. Hardik Pandya removed Saim Ayub off the very first legal delivery of the match after beginning with a wide, setting the tone for Pakistan's struggles. Jasprit Bumrah then dismissed Mohammad Haris to leave Pakistan reeling at 6/2 within the powerplay.

The spin duo of Kuldeep Yadav and Axar Patel then took control of the middle overs. Kuldeep delivered a masterclass performance with figures of 3/18 in 4 overs, while Axar Patel complemented him perfectly with 2/18 in 4 overs, choking the scoring rate and picking up crucial wickets.

Sahibzada Farhan stood as Pakistan's lone warrior with a fighting 40 off 44 balls, decorated with one four and three sixes. A late cameo from Shaheen Shah Afridi (33* off 14 balls) provided some respectability to the total, but Pakistan's batting unit had largely crumbled on a track that offered little excuse for their collapse.

India's Clinical Chase

Abhishek Sharma set the tone for India's chase with an explosive 31 off 13 balls, taking the attack to the Pakistani bowlers from the outset. The chase was then expertly guided by captain Suryakumar Yadav, who remained unbeaten on 47 off 37 balls, showcasing his class and composure under pressure.

Tilak Varma provided valuable support in the middle order, ensuring India never lost momentum during the chase. The target of 128 was never threatening for a batting lineup full of power-hitters, and India completed the victory with 25 deliveries to spare.

Key Performances

Kuldeep Yadav emerged as the Player of the Match with his exceptional spell of left-arm chinaman bowling. His ability to extract turn and maintain pressure in the middle overs proved decisive in Pakistan's downfall. Axar Patel's economical bowling complemented Kuldeep perfectly, forming a formidable spin partnership.

From the batting perspective, Suryakumar Yadav's captaincy and batting performance stood out, as he marshaled his resources expertly and anchored the chase with a mature innings.

Tournament Implications

This comprehensive victory all but confirmed India's qualification for the Super Four stage of the Asia Cup 2025. With two wins from two matches and a massive net run rate of +4.793, India topped Group A convincingly.

For Pakistan, the defeat put pressure on their remaining group stage matches, though they eventually secured qualification with a victory over UAE despite the ongoing controversy surrounding the handshake incident.

The Handshake Controversy

The match was overshadowed by a diplomatic incident when Indian players refused to shake hands with Pakistani players following the match. This led to Pakistan appealing to the International Cricket Council and requesting the removal of match referee Andy Pycroft, though the ICC rejected both requests.

Conclusion

India's dominant performance against Pakistan showcased their superiority in all departments of the game. The victory demonstrated India's depth in both bowling and batting, with the team continuing their excellent form since their T20 World Cup triumph.

  • Total domination from India in all three departments of cricket
  • Kuldeep Yadav and Axar Patel's spin mastery dismantled Pakistan's batting
  • Suryakumar Yadav's composed leadership guided India to an easy victory
  • India's qualification to Super Four stage virtually secured
